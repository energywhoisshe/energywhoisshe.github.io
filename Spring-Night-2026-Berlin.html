Songs of Invitation / 초대하는 소리

Performed by Ven.Donghwan, Cha Yeonså, in play with the Audience / 퍼포먼스 동환스님, 차연서, 프루이 관객과 함께

We call to you, all gathered here, with one whole heart. Oh names that never answer, no matter how we cry. Come gently now, drifting on spring winds, on flower winds. Is this a dream, is this an illusion? How helplessly war and massacre repeat themselves. How helplessly silence repeats itself here, where the Holocaust once passed. / We call to the spirits of this day with one whole heart. As we live, there is never a day without birth, nor a day without departure. In homes, on streets, in buildings, in bathrooms, in the mountains, at sea, in the military — by suicide, by shattered glass, by acid burns, struck by cars, choked into silence. Even after death, stripped and desecrated. The body that was yours, suddenly gone. Whether beaten to death or left to rot, human or animal. What can be done, who can truly know the desperation and urgency of a single breath? / Spirit of this day, oh wandering spirit. This day, in this brief moment of prayer, offered with all sincerity, may you set down all hurt, all wrongs, all suffering. May you meet a better world and kinder bonds in the next life.

As I was born all of a sudden, so too I may leave. Though I was not ready to leave, I will set down my desire for revenge to end this endless cycle of karma. Like a young dog, deaf and blind, nose to the earth, licking the ground, following the wind to find the way, walking softly through the cool air and returning home. So too, with courage and calm, I will walk the way I must.

Merit upon merit, all merits long gathered. Fulfilled upon fulfilled, may all be shared. Assisted by the powers of compassion and vow You swiftly cross the river, into the arms of boundless light. What a relief. Maha Prajna Paramita. Maha Prajna Paramita. Maha Prajna Paramita.

(Hwacheong of Spring Night. Lyrics by Cha Yeonså, Composition and Consultation by Ven.Donghwan.)

2025년 12월 서울에서 세 시간 동안 열린 <봄밤>이 2026년 5월 베를린, 봄의 끝자락에서 반복된다. 이번 퍼포먼스는 다성적 목소리의 소란스러운 현존을 불러일으키며, 그것을 눈꺼풀을 내린 존재들의 침묵으로 전환한다. 그것은 태어날 때부터 들리지 않고 보이지 않는 어린 개의 선천적인 용기 및 평화로움과 동행한다.

제목 <봄밤>은 김언희 시인의 동명의 시에서 가져온 것이다. 이 시에 등장하는 주먹만 한 백골과 흰 꽃들은, 어미도, 새끼도, 주인도, 독자도 없이, 이미 발라먹힌 채 남겨진 빈 몸으로서 열린다. 그 안에 남는 것은 비극이 아니라—마침내 맞고 잠든 후에야 도달하는 기쁨 뿐이다.

— 글 차연서, 2026

First performed in Seoul in 2025, the midwinter, three hours — Spring Night recurs in Berlin in 2026, at the tail end of spring. This iteration calls the restless presence of polyphonic voices, giving way to the silence of those who have lowered their eyelids. It moves in companionship with the native courage and peacefulness of a young dog, born deaf and blind.

The title Spring Night (봄밤) is borrowed from a poem of the same name by Kim Eon Hee. The fist-sized bones and white flowers in this poem, open as empty bodies that have been devoured, with no mother, no offspring, no owner, no reader. What remains is not tragedy but joy, finally being struck and falling into sleep.

- Writing by Cha Yeonså, May 2026.

Spring Night

Sun 31.5.2026, 16:00 / HAU1
Performance / Korean, English / With English subtitles / 1 hour

“Spring Night” develops from the motif of the “dog beneath the magnolia tree – miscarrying, beaten, and finally asleep”, drawn from a poem by South Korean poet Kim Eon Hee. The Berlin version, following its premiere in the depths of winter in Seoul, unfolds as a ritual in late spring – something that arrives from afar and is already in the process of disappearing. In this one-hour interactive performance, voices and sounds intertwine with fragments of poetry, deconstructed vocalizations, and Buddhist chanting. Here, loneliness appears as a shared condition: each person remains alone within their own perception, yet is gathered within a collective rhythm of attention and movement.
